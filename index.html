Our Mission

The Computational Social Science Laboratory, commonly known as the Harris-Ivanov Laboratory, is a key interdisciplinary research unit within the Department of Supercomputers and Quantum Informatics, part of the Faculty of Computational Mathematics and Cybernetics at Suaolamanabaar National University.

Our mission is to explore the fundamental connections between human cognition, social structures, and complex systems. We develop novel computational methods to analyze anthropological data, aiming to build quantitative models that explain the dynamics of cultural evolution and social behavior, particularly in isolated or extreme environments.

Research Projects

Digital Social Dynamics

Using the Suaolamanabaar Special Administrative Region, an arctic 'data city', as our primary sample, we study the evolution of human behavior patterns, social structures, and cultural identity in highly automated and networked isolated societies.

Human-Machine Cognitive Interaction

We explore the interaction between human operators and complex information systems in extreme environments, focusing on decision-making processes, cognitive load, and the development of adaptive user interfaces.

Computational Models of Cultural Transmission

This project focuses on creating agent-based models to simulate the spread and evolution of ideas, norms, and beliefs within different social network structures.

Our History

The lab was born from a chance encounter on the Siberian ice fields. In the mid-1990s, Stanley Harris, then a doctoral researcher, met his guide, Andrei Ivanov. Two brilliant young men, both facing hardship, formed a deep friendship out of a shared thirst for knowledge.

After the collapse of the USSR, previously classified data on large-scale social experiments began to surface. Andrei, with his unique experience growing up in remote Siberian communities, saw patterns and "cognitive gaps" where traditional analysis had failed. Stanley, meanwhile, became fascinated by this grand scientific narrative that had collapsed due to flawed models. The two joined forces, founding the laboratory in the early 21st century, determined to find a spark of truth in the ashes of failure.
